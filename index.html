About

I’m U.P. Nguyen (she/her), currently working in data and platform engineering while pursuing a graduate degree at New York University. My interests sit at the intersection of climate science, data, and public policy. I like to think I've built a life centered around movement, mentorship, and access. My background spans software, experiential education, coaching, and community work, and I’m most energized when I’m helping people feel more capable, connected, and empowered.

I earned my degree in computer science from Brown University in 2019, where I also explored environmental sciences, economics, and the arts. Since then, I’ve worked across cloud security, data platforms, CI/CD, and internal tooling, first at Bose, now at Brooks, while continuing to invest deeply in the human side of leadership and learning.

In 2022, I made a deliberate pivot to work as an instructor at Outward Bound. That season clarified something important for me: I want a career that makes room for both technical rigor and soul work. These days, that looks like building good systems by day and guiding, coaching, and creating meaningful outdoor experiences beyond the screen.

I’m a proudly queer, first-generation Vietnamese-American and first-generation college graduate. Across all parts of my life, I care deeply about equity, belonging, and expanding access, whether that means improving a platform, mentoring a runner, or helping more people find themselves outdoors.

Outside of work, you’ll usually find me running, coaching, climbing, backpacking, cooking, or writing. You can also browse my Strava, see what I’m making in the kitchen, or read more on my Substack.
